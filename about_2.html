About Me

This is a page about what I like to do when I'm not doing maths or computers. Because sometimes I don't do maths and computers and do other things instead.

Art and Photography

Over the last couple of years I've done little bits of photography. Film's my preference when I'm doing personal photos, I have a a Pentax K1000, which I picked up pretty cheap in Bristol. Previously I've done a few events using digital, including some live music and at one point an opera. Although not particularly good I enjoy it and try to keep a camera with me most places I go. I let it slide a little bit when I was doing my Master's but I'm trying to pick it up a bit more now I've got a bit more time on my hands.

In terms of art, I've never really done much before or taken lessons, I just thought it would be something fun to try. I've done a few paintings and fiddled around with different mediums (acrylic, watercolour, pencil...) but don't really know what I'm doing. Now I'm working and have a bit more time it's definitely something I want to put more effort into!

Sports and Cycling

I like keeping fit as well! My favourite sport is Muay Thai kickboxing, where I captained my university team and fought competitively. Fighting was kind of fun but I didn't really enjoy having my face bust up that much, so took some steps back to coach. Taking a team of people to different places and cornering for them was a really interesting experience. I've been training on and off for about five years now, and did MMA before that. Again, my training took a bit of a back seat during my studies, but I've really enjoyed getting back into it.

My other passion is bikes. I can't really afford a flashy carbon one, but I love restoring and riding old models. My favourite one is my dad's classic Peugeout, as my 21st birthday present I got some money towards restoring it. I sprayed the frame black and got some new parts as well as a custom headbadge I really love. More recently I did up an old single speed someone was selling out of their garage in Bristol, which I cycle to and from my job in London. In terms of cycling achievements I've crossed France by bike, as well as climbing Mt. Ventoux of Tour de France fame.

Travelling

I guess the final thing to say is that I just really like trying new things. I've travelled bits and bobs, last year I went to Spain for a little and worked on a farm and as a gardener in the south which was cool. I'm not a massively manual kind of guy, but it was definitely an experience. More recently I just came back from Philadelphia. I'd been when I was 16 to go skating, but it was good to go back. Something I'm looking forward to now I'm finished uni is being able to afford to go to more interesting places, so watch this space!
